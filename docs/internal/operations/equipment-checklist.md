## Weekly maintenance schedule

| Interval | Task |
|---|---|
| **Every job day** | Check pump oil + engine oil level; flush pump with clean water after SH use; rinse downstream injector; drain hoses; check for leaks |
| **Weekly** | **Pump oil** — inspect, top up; change per manufacturer hours (often first ~50 hrs, then per manual). **Inlet water filter** — clean/replace. **Chemical injector filter** — clean. **O-rings** on quick-connects/tips — inspect, replace cracked ones (keep a pack). Check high-pressure hose for bulges/cuts. |
| **Monthly** | Engine oil change per hours; air filter clean/replace; spark plug check; inspect unloader and nozzles for wear (worn tips = lost pressure). |
| **Winterizing (NJ, before hard freeze)** | Never leave water in the pump over a freeze. Run **pump antifreeze / RV antifreeze** through the pump until it comes out the outlet, or use a compressed-air blow-out. Store SH indoors (freezes and degrades). Drain hoses. A cracked pump from freezing is the most common avoidable off-season failure. |

## Field failure quick-fix table (2-minute diagnostics)

| Symptom | Likely cause | 2-minute fix |
|---|---|---|
| **Pump losing pressure / pulsing** | Worn or wrong tip; air in system; low water supply; clogged inlet filter; failing unloader | Check tip isn't worn (swap in a known-good tip). Purge air: run with trigger open. Confirm full water flow. Clean inlet filter. Pulsing often = worn tip or unloader. |
| **Unloader issues (pressure spikes on trigger release / won't build)** | Stuck/worn unloader valve or bad seal | With engine off, work the trigger to bleed pressure; check unloader isn't stuck. Adjust unloader per manual. If seals worn, swap unloader (carry spare if you can). |
| **Tip clog (weak/split spray)** | Debris in nozzle | Trigger off, remove tip, clear with **tip cleaner wire** or blow backward; never look into the tip. Reseat, retest. |
| **Hose burst / leak at coupler** | Failed hose or bad **o-ring** | Kill engine, release pressure. Replace o-ring (carry a pack) or swap to **spare high-pressure hose**. Teflon tape threaded fittings. |
| **No water flow** | Spigot off/low; kinked feed hose; clogged inlet filter; air lock | Confirm spigot fully open and pressure adequate; walk the feed hose for kinks; clean inlet screen; purge air with trigger open, no tip. |
| **Engine won't start** | Out of gas; choke/throttle wrong; stale fuel; fouled plug; low-oil sensor cutoff | Check fuel level. Set choke ON + throttle to start position. **Low-oil sensor** stops many engines — check oil level first. Pull firmly. If flooded, wait, choke off. Check/replace spark plug. |

**Golden rule:** Kill the engine and release trigger pressure before touching any fitting, tip, or hose.
