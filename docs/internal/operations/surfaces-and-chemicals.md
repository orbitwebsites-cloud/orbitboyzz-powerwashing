## Surface-by-surface guide

### Concrete driveway
- **Pressure:** 3,000–4,000 PSI **with a surface cleaner** (preferred). Wanding: **25° green**, or 15° yellow for tough spots.
- **Mix:** Pre-treat with **~1–2% SH** (1 gal 12.5% SH : 5–11 gal water). **Oil spots: degreaser** applied direct, agitate.
- **Dwell:** SH/cleaner 5–10 min, keep wet. Degreaser 5–10 min.
- **Rinse:** Flush toward street/storm drain, full surface, no puddled chem.
- **#1 amateur damage:** **Zebra striping** — uneven wand distance/speed. Fix by using a surface cleaner and overlapping every pass; never stop mid-pass. Post-treat evens tone.

### Pavers (concrete/clay)
- **Pressure:** **1,500–2,500 PSI**, surface cleaner on low, or **25° green**. Keep tip back.
- **Mix:** Same SH pre-treat as concrete. Degreaser on oil.
- **Dwell:** 5–10 min, keep wet.
- **Rinse:** Rinse fully; **re-sand joints** afterward if polymeric sand was blown out.
- **#1 amateur damage:** **Blasting out joint sand** with too-close/too-high pressure, causing shifting/weeds. Keep distance, angle low.

### Vinyl siding — **SOFT WASH ONLY** (most requested)
- **Pressure:** **Soft wash — ~100–300 PSI at the nozzle** (black/65° or soft-wash tip). **Never high pressure** — it drives water behind siding.
- **Mix (target 1–2% SH at surface):**
  - Light/regular: **1 gal 12.5% SH : 6–8 gal water** (~1.5%) + surfactant.
  - Heavier algae/mildew: **1 gal 12.5% SH : 3–4 gal water** (~2.5–3%) + surfactant.
  - Add **surfactant** (e.g. "house wash" surfactant) ~a few oz per batch so it clings and stays wet.
- **Dwell:** 5–10 min. **Keep it wet — never let SH dry on siding** (streaks/oxidation reaction).
- **Rinse:** Apply low, **bottom-up**; **rinse top-down** thoroughly. Rinse windows/trim/doors.
- **#1 amateur damage:** **Forcing water behind siding / under laps** with high pressure → moisture intrusion, mold inside walls. Also letting mix dry = streaks. Soft wash only.

### Brick
- **Pressure:** **Soft wash preferred.** If pressure, **1,000–1,500 PSI max**, 25–40°, keep distance.
- **Mix:** ~1–2% SH for organic growth; keep off fresh mortar.
- **Dwell:** 5–10 min, keep wet.
- **Rinse:** Thorough, top-down.
- **#1 amateur damage:** **Blowing out mortar joints** and driving water into porous brick. Low pressure, let chemistry work.

### Stucco / EIFS
- **Pressure:** **Soft wash only.** Treat as fragile. If any pressure, **<1,500 PSI** at distance with 40° — but soft wash is the safe default.
- **Mix:** ~1–2% SH, surfactant, gentle.
- **Dwell:** 5–10 min, keep wet.
- **Rinse:** Gentle, thorough.
- **#1 amateur damage:** **Chipping/pitting the stucco surface** and forcing water into the wall system. No high pressure, ever.

### Wood deck
- **Pressure:** **500–1,200 PSI**, **40° white**, move **with the grain**, tip kept back and moving.
- **Mix:** Mild SH (~1% or a dedicated wood cleaner); some prefer percarbonate wood cleaners. Rinse well.
- **Dwell:** 5–10 min, keep wet, don't let dry.
- **Rinse:** With the grain, even.
- **#1 amateur damage:** **Furring/gouging the soft grain** — stopping in one spot or too-high pressure raises fibers. Keep moving, low pressure, with grain.

### Composite deck (Trex-type)
- **Pressure:** **Soft wash / low pressure preferred.** If pressure, **≤1,500 PSI, 40° fan, keep the tip 8+ inches back** and follow the board manufacturer's limit.
- **Mix:** Mild soap or ~1% SH for mildew; rinse well.
- **Dwell:** 5–10 min.
- **Rinse:** Even, with the board direction.
- **#1 amateur damage:** **Etching/scarring the composite cap** with close high pressure — permanent, not repairable. Keep distance, use fan tip.

### Concrete patio
- **Pressure:** **3,000–4,000 PSI with surface cleaner**, or **25° green** wand. Same method as driveway.
- **Mix:** ~1–2% SH pre/post-treat; degreaser on grill grease/food stains.
- **Dwell:** 5–10 min, keep wet.
- **Rinse:** Full, uniform.
- **#1 amateur damage:** **Zebra striping** — use surface cleaner, overlap passes, post-treat to even.

### Vinyl / wood fence — **SOFT WASH ONLY**
- **Pressure:** **Soft wash — ~100–300 PSI at the nozzle** (black/65° or soft-wash tip) to clean. For a wood fence, **rinse with 40° white, moving with the grain**, tip kept back and moving. Never high pressure on either.
- **Mix (target ~1–2% SH at surface — same as siding):** 1 gal 12.5% SH : 6–8 gal water (~1.5%) + surfactant; step to 1 : 3–4 (~2.5–3%) for the heavy algae on the shaded side.
- **Dwell:** 5–10 min, keep wet — **never let SH dry** (streaks).
- **Rinse:** Rinse **both sides** thoroughly; on wood, rinse **with the grain**. Pre-wet and re-rinse the plantings along the fence line.
- **#1 amateur damage:** **High pressure furring wood pickets and blowing vinyl panels off their rails.** Vinyl fence panels float in the rails and pop out; wood grain raises and splinters. Soft wash to clean, gentle 40° rinse only.

### Trash bin cleaning (procedure)
- **Prep:** **Bins must be empty** — schedule right after the customer's pickup day. Bag and set aside any stuck solid residual before washing.
- **Wash:** **Degrease + wash interior and exterior** — bin brush + degreaser cuts the grease and stuck-on gunk. Get the lid, rim, and handles.
- **Disinfect + deodorize:** After washing, apply a **disinfectant and deodorizer** so it comes back sanitary and smell-free, not just rinsed.
- **Drain + dry:** **Tip the bin to drain**, then let it **air-dry** before returning it upright (lid cracked open).
- **Wash-water disposal (hard rule):** **NEVER let bin wash water run into the storm drain** — it's food/organic waste and it's not allowed. Drain to **grass/landscaping** or the customer's **sanitary sewer cleanout where permitted**.
- **PPE / cleanup:** **Glove up.** **Bag any residual waste and drop it back into the now-clean bin** (into the customer's liner/bag) — leave nothing behind on their property.

## SH mix quick-reference (12.5% sodium hypochlorite stock)

| Target at surface | SH : Water | Typical use |
|---|---|---|
| ~1% | 1 : 11 | Light siding, gentle surfaces |
| ~1.5% | 1 : 7 | Standard house wash |
| ~2% | 1 : 5 | Heavier siding, concrete pre-treat |
| ~3% (max) | 1 : 3 | Heavy algae/mildew — **do not exceed** |

**Always add surfactant** to house-wash mixes so it clings and stays wet. **Above ~3% SH risks plant kill, siding oxidation, and fumes — don't go higher.**

## Plant & landscaping protection protocol (every job)

1. **Pre-wet** all plants, shrubs, grass, and beds in and around the work zone with plain water **before** any chemical. Wet foliage dilutes any overspray.
2. **Cover** delicate/prized plantings with plastic sheeting or tarps where practical.
3. **Keep them wet** during the job — re-wet if the sun is drying them.
4. **Rinse after** — flood all foliage and soil with plain water when you finish that section, and again before you leave.
5. If you see wilting, **rinse heavily** and tell the customer immediately (see incident procedure).

## Safety section

### Sodium hypochlorite (SH) handling
- **PPE:** Chemical-resistant gloves, **eye protection (goggles/face shield)**, closed shoes, and clothes you don't mind bleaching. Rinse skin/eyes immediately with water on contact.
- **NEVER mix SH with other chemicals.** Specifically:
  - **SH + ammonia → toxic chloramine gas.**
  - **SH + acid (e.g. muriatic/brick acid, some rust removers) → chlorine gas.**
  Both can seriously injure or kill in an enclosed space. **Only ever dilute SH with plain water and add approved surfactant.** Keep acids and ammonia products completely separate.
- Store SH in a cool, shaded, vented spot; it off-gasses. Label every container. Keep away from kids/pets.
- Work with airflow; avoid breathing fumes. Don't apply in a way that mists back onto you.

### Ladder rules (solo operator)
- **Prefer to stay on the ground** — soft wash + extension wand reaches most 2-story work. That's the point of the reach setup.
- **No 3-story work** and **no roofs** — out of scope, out of safe reach for a solo op.
- If a ladder is unavoidable: level, stable footing; **3 points of contact**; **never spray a running high-pressure wand from a ladder** (reaction force can throw you). Only do controlled soft-wash application from a fully stable ladder, and only when necessary.
- Solo means no spotter — if it feels sketchy, don't. Use the wand from the ground instead.

### Electrical
- **Avoid outdoor outlets, light fixtures, doorbells, cameras, panels, and the A/C condenser.** Cover or spray around them.
- Keep spray away from meter boxes and any exposed wiring.
- Don't run your machine or plug tools into questionable outdoor circuits. Assume outdoor outlets can be live and wet = danger.

### Weather that cancels or pauses a job
- **Rain during/right after** application washes chem off before it works — reschedule or wait.
- **High wind** (drift onto plants, cars, neighbors, yourself) — pause SH work.
- **Cold: below ~40°F / near freezing** — SH works poorly, and freezing risks equipment; skip or reschedule. Winterize if hard freeze coming (see `equipment-checklist.md`).
- **Extreme heat / direct sun** — chem dries too fast on the surface; work small sections, keep everything wet, or reschedule the hottest part of the day.
- **Lightning** — stop immediately.
