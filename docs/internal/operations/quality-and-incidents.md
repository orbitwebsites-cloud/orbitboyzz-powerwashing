## Post-job quality checklist — what "done" looks like

### Driveway (concrete/pavers)
- [ ] **No zebra striping** — tone is even across the whole slab
- [ ] No missed strips, edges, or corners; expansion joints cleaned
- [ ] Oil spots addressed (or customer told if a stain is permanent)
- [ ] Paver joints intact / re-sanded if sand was disturbed
- [ ] Rinsed clean toward drain; no chem film or foam left
- [ ] No etching or wand marks

### House wash (soft wash siding) — most requested
- [ ] **No missed high sections** — check the top course, peaks, gable ends, under eaves
- [ ] Even, clean tone across all four sides; no drip streaks
- [ ] **Windows rinsed** — no white chemical residue or spotting
- [ ] Trim, doors, fixtures, gutters exterior rinsed of residue
- [ ] No SH dried onto any surface
- [ ] No plant burn — beds and grass rinsed, no wilting

### Patio (concrete/pavers)
- [ ] Even tone, no striping
- [ ] Edges/corners and joints done
- [ ] Furniture, grill, mats **moved back** where they were
- [ ] Rinsed clean, no chem residue

### Every job — before you call it done
- [ ] **No chemical residue on plants** — foliage and soil rinsed
- [ ] **Everything moved back** — furniture, planters, mats, vehicles clear
- [ ] Outlets/fixtures/cameras undamaged and dry
- [ ] Hoses coiled, water off, gates as found
- [ ] **"After" photos taken** of every surface

## Redo policy

**If the customer flags anything during the walkthrough, fix it on the spot — always. No debate, no upcharge.** You're already set up and it's cheaper than a bad review or a callback. Re-treat, re-rinse, re-pass until they're satisfied with what's in scope. (Out-of-scope requests: politely note them and offer a separate quote.)

## Incident procedure (something breaks or gets damaged)

Follow the order every time:

1. **Stay calm.** Stop work. Don't argue, don't hide it, don't guess at blame.
2. **Photograph** the damage immediately — wide shot + close-up. Compare against your **"before" photos** (this is exactly why you take them).
3. **Tell the customer immediately and honestly** — in person, before you leave: *"I want to show you something that happened / that I found."* Never let them discover it after you've gone.
4. **Offer the fix** — repair, replace, bring in a pro, or deduct from the bill. Make it right. For anything beyond a small fix, get their contact and follow up in writing.
5. **Log it** in the incident log below (and notify your insurance for anything significant).

**Pre-existing vs. caused:** If your "before" photos show it was already there, calmly show the customer the timestamped photo. This is the whole reason for the arrival walkthrough — no photo, no defense.
