## PHASE 1 — Arrival protocol (10 min) — DO NOT SKIP

**Text customer "~15 min out" before you leave the prior stop.**

1. **Park** — on the street or at the curb, not blocking the customer or neighbors. Never park on a surface you're about to wash.
2. **Knock / announce** — introduce yourself: *"Hi, [you] with Orbit PowerWash, here for your [service]."* Confirm scope out loud: *"Today I'm doing [driveway / house wash / patio]. Anything changed since we booked?"*
3. **Walk the property WITH your phone — take "before" photos of EVERY surface you'll touch.** This is your #1 protection against damage claims.
   - Photograph each surface **and every pre-existing defect**: oxidation/chalking on siding, cracked concrete, chipped pavers, loose trim, torn screens, existing stains, dented gutters, wobbly light fixtures, damaged window seals.
   - Narrate to the customer as you go: *"Just documenting the starting condition — see this crack here and this faded spot? That's already there, I'll show you the after too."*
   - Note **water source** location and **outdoor outlets/fixtures**.
   - Confirm **vehicles moved**, **windows/doors closed**, **pets inside**, **gates unlocked**.
4. **Set expectations** — tell them the realistic on-site time and that you'll do a walkthrough together at the end and collect payment on the spot.

## PHASE 2 — Setup

**Driveway (~10 min) / House wash (~15–20 min)**

- [ ] Connect to spigot, purge air, check for hose leaks.
- [ ] Stage hoses along the planned path; avoid trip lines across walkways.
- [ ] **Plant/landscaping protection** — pre-wet all beds, shrubs, and grass along the work zone; cover sensitive plantings. Full protocol in `surfaces-and-chemicals.md`.
- [ ] Cover/avoid outdoor outlets, A/C condenser, light fixtures, doorbells/cameras.
- [ ] Mix chemical to the correct ratio in a clearly labeled container. PPE on before handling SH.
- [ ] Prime machine, confirm pressure, select correct tip (see `surfaces-and-chemicals.md`).

## PHASE 3 — Work order per service

### Driveway washing (concrete/pavers) — work time ~30–45 min
1. Pre-wet the surface.
2. Apply cleaning/SH solution; **spot-treat oil stains with degreaser** and let dwell.
3. Dwell per chem sheet (do not let it dry on the surface — re-wet if it starts drying).
4. **Surface cleaner pass** (if available) in overlapping straight lines — this is what prevents **zebra striping**. If wanding, keep the tip a **consistent distance and angle**, overlap every pass, never stop mid-stripe.
5. Detail edges/cracks with a wand tip.
6. **Post-treat** for uniform appearance if needed; rinse toward the street/drain.

### House washing (soft wash siding) — work time ~90–150 min — MOST REQUESTED
1. Work **bottom-up when applying** SH mix (prevents clean-line streaks on dry siding), **rinse top-down**.
2. Apply soft-wash mix low-pressure to one section at a time.
3. **Dwell** per chem sheet — keep it wet, do not let it dry.
4. Agitate stubborn spots with a soft brush (no pressure into siding seams).
5. Rinse thoroughly top-down; **rinse windows, trim, doors, and fixtures** so no residue dries.
6. Move around the house section by section; **keep landscaping rinsed** as you go.

### Patio washing (concrete/pavers) — work time ~20–40 min
Same as driveway method, sized to the patio. Watch for adjacent siding, furniture, grill — move or cover.
