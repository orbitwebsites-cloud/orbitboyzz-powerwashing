# Door-Knocking Playbook — Plainsboro & West Windsor

Your #1 channel this week: **~6 sessions × 40 doors ≈ 12 quotes ≈ 6 bookings.** Everything below is built to hit that.

---

## Legal & etiquette (NJ suburbs) — do this right

- **Best hours:** weekdays **4–7pm** (home from work), weekends **10am–1pm** (outside doing yard work). Never before 9am or after dusk. Stop knocking at sunset.
- **No-knock lists / "No Soliciting" signs:** New Jersey towns maintain no-knock registries and many HOAs post rules. **If a door or a community entrance says "No Soliciting," skip it — no exceptions.** If a homeowner says "we're on the no-knock list," apologize, thank them, leave. Don't argue; it's not worth a complaint.
- **Peddler permits:** Some NJ municipalities technically require a permit for door-to-door solicitation. You are offering a service and booking estimates, but to stay clean: keep it low-key, be off private property the moment someone declines, and if a resident or officer asks you to leave, leave immediately and politely. Carry ID.
- **HOA/gated sections:** Don't enter gated communities or clubhouses. Work the open, public-street neighborhoods.
- **Presentation:** clean clothes, a branded shirt if you have one (a plain polo is fine), phone in hand, friendly and quick. You are the whole company — look like someone they'd let near their house.
- **Dogs, kids, packages:** don't lean past thresholds, don't step onto porches uninvited, back up a step when the door opens.

## How to pick streets

You're not knocking randomly. You're hunting **visible dirt**.

1. **Target the eyesores.** Green/black algae streaks on siding, dark mildew on north-facing walls, driveways with tire stains, mossy pavers, black-spotted patios. A dirty house is a warm lead before you say a word.
2. **Cluster, don't scatter.** Work one street fully, then the next. Tight clusters = less walking, and the neighbor special (below) only works when neighbors can see each other's driveways.
3. **Cluster around any booked job — this is the money move.** When you have a job on a street, knock the **6–10 nearest doors** the day of or day before. "I'm doing your neighbor's driveway at #14 tomorrow" is the single strongest opener you have.
4. **Age of neighborhood:** established Plainsboro/West Windsor subdivisions (10+ year-old homes) have more built-up algae and dirtier concrete than brand-new builds. Prioritize them.
5. **Skip:** brand-new construction, obvious rentals with absentee owners, and anything freshly power-washed already.

Scout 2–3 streets the evening before and note house numbers with the worst dirt. Hit those first while your energy is high.

## The knock script (word for word)

Keep it under 20 seconds before you pause. Smile, take a half-step back after they open.

> **You:** "Hi, sorry to bother you — I'm [Name] with Orbit PowerWash, I'm local, just started up cleaning driveways and houses here in [Plainsboro/West Windsor]. I noticed your **[driveway's got some tire staining / siding's got that green algae on the north side]** and figured I'd introduce myself. I'm giving free quotes this week — want me to take a quick look while I'm here?"

Then **stop talking.** Let them answer. Branch from there.

### Branch A — Interested ("Sure" / "How much?" / "Yeah, what do you do?")

> **You:** "Awesome. So I do three things — driveways, patios, and house washing. The houses I do with a **soft wash**, low pressure and a cleaning solution so it lifts the algae without blasting your siding or paint. Concrete and pavers I can turn the pressure up on. What's been bugging you most — the driveway, the house, or the patio?"

Look at what they point to. Then:

> **You:** "Got it. Let me measure it real quick and I'll text you an exact number in a few minutes — no obligation. What's the best number to reach you?"

**Get the phone number.** That's the win at the door — a number and a service they want. Log it as a lead on the spot (`lead-tracker.md`), text them the quote before you leave the street (pricing in `sales/pricing-guide.md`), and pitch the neighbor special below.

If they want to book right there: pull up your calendar and set a day. Confirm number + address. Log as **booked**.

### Branch B — Not now ("We're good" / "Maybe later" / "Send me info")

> **You:** "Totally fair. Can I grab your number or give you mine? I'm doing founding-customer pricing for my first ten jobs this week, so if you decide the driveway's bugging you, text me and I'll lock you in at that rate. Either way — I'm Orbit PowerWash, (609) 297-7412."

- If they'll give a number: log as a **new** lead, follow up per the tracker rules.
- If not: leave your number (see leave-behind below). Don't push. Move on — your time is worth more at the next door.

### Branch C — Not home

Don't skip it — this is a lead you can still catch.
- Leave your name + number (leave-behind below).
- Log the address as **new / not-home** in the tracker so you can re-knock on a different day/time.
- If the house is visibly dirty, mark it a priority re-knock for the weekend daytime session.

### Awkward-question quick answers

- **"Are you insured/licensed?"** → "I'm a local owner-operator just launching — I treat every house carefully, soft wash on siding so nothing gets damaged. Happy to walk you through exactly what I'll do before I start." (Speak to real coverage per your actual policy; don't claim what you don't have.)
- **"How are you so cheap / are you legit?"** → "I'm new and building my reputation, so I'm doing founding-customer pricing for my first ten jobs this week. I'd rather have ten happy neighbors telling people about me than charge full rate to strangers."
- **"I'll think about it."** → "No problem — want me to text you the quote so it's in your phone? Zero obligation." (Get the number.)

## The neighbor special (your best close)

Frame it honestly — it's real economics, and saying so builds trust:

> "One thing — if a neighbor or two books the same day I come out, I take a bit off everyone's price. It saves me from driving back out, so I pass that on. If you want to grab the person next door or across the street, I'll do you both at the neighbor rate."

Why it works: it turns one yes into two or three on the same street, cuts your drive time, and makes the customer your salesperson. Whenever you book a job, **immediately knock the neighbors** and say "I'm already going to be right here at #__ — want me to do yours the same day at the neighbor rate?" (Neighbor-rate pricing: `sales/pricing-guide.md`.)

## Leave-behind with no printing budget

You have no flyers — you don't need them.

1. **Text-on-the-spot (best):** if they give a number, text them right there: *"Great meeting you — [Name] w/ Orbit PowerWash. I'll send your quote shortly. (609) 297-7412 / orbitpowerwash.com"* Now they have your number and can't lose a flyer.
2. **Pen + paper:** keep a small pad. Write **"Orbit PowerWash — (609) 297-7412 — orbitpowerwash.com"** and hand it over. Costs nothing, feels personal.
3. **Not-home doors:** write the same on a scrap and wedge it in the door (don't put anything *inside* a mailbox — that's a federal no-no). Or just log it and re-knock.

---

## Log every door

Every door goes in the tracker (`lead-tracker.md`) — yes, even not-homes. On your phone, one row each:

- **Interested / got number** → status **quoted** (once you send the number) or **new**.
- **Not now, gave number** → status **new**, set a follow-up date.
- **Not home** → status **new / not-home**, flag for re-knock.
- **Hard no / no-knock** → don't log a re-knock; just move on.

At the end of each session, count doors knocked and quotes given and drop them in the daily scorecard (`00-week-one-sprint.md`). Target every session: **40 doors → 2 quotes → 1 booking.**
