## How local ranking actually works

Google ranks local businesses on **three factors**. You can't buy any of them; you earn them.

1. **Proximity** — how close you are to the person searching. You don't control this, and it's why you rank differently in Plainsboro than in Ewing. A service-area business (you) ranks best near the center of the areas you list — one more reason to list all three towns correctly.
2. **Relevance** — how well your profile and site match what they typed. You control this: list all five services by name, use the town names in your profile, site, posts, and Q&A, and pick the right categories.
3. **Prominence** — how known and trusted you look. This is **reviews, citations, links, and activity.** It's where the game is actually won, and **reviews are the biggest lever you have.**

**Two different results — know the difference:**
- **The map pack** (the top 3 businesses shown with the map) — driven mostly by your **Google Business Profile + reviews + proximity.** This is where a new local wins fastest.
- **Organic results** (the blue links below the map) — driven by your **website** (town pages, content, FAQ). Slower, but the town landing pages a separate agent is building aim here.

**Nobody can guarantee #1.** Anyone who does is lying — Google's ranking isn't for sale, and proximity alone means you'll never be #1 for everyone everywhere. What actually moves you up: **more real reviews, faster; a complete, active GBP; consistent NAP everywhere; and genuinely useful town pages.** That's the whole list. Ignore anyone selling "SEO packages."

For a local service business, your **Google Business Profile (GBP)** does more work than your website. Setup is already covered in `free-channels.md` — ranking it is about keeping it **complete, active, and full of fresh reviews.**

- [ ] NAP exactly as the one-pager: **Orbit PowerWash · (609) 297-7412 · orbitpowerwash.com**. Service-area business — **no storefront address.**

**Feed it every week — activity is itself a ranking signal:**
- [ ] **Photos, weekly minimum.** Upload before/after shots from real jobs. Name the files and captions with the town + service ("house-washing-west-windsor.jpg", caption "Soft wash in Plainsboro"). Profiles with fresh photos get far more calls.
- [ ] **Google Posts** — post one update a week (a recent job, a seasonal tip, the founding offer). Free, and it tells Google the profile is alive.
- [ ] **Q&A seeding** — you're allowed to ask and answer your own questions. Seed 5–8: "Do you service Ewing?" "Is house washing safe for vinyl siding?" "Do you clean trash bins?" Answer them in your voice — it puts your keywords and towns right on the profile.

**Above all — REVIEW VELOCITY.** This is the single biggest lever in the whole playbook.
- Reviews — how many, how recent, and how often new ones arrive — move map-pack rank more than anything else you control.
- **The review-ask system is already built:** the same-day review ask in `lead-tracker.md` and the text in `ready-to-post.md`. That system *is* the SEO engine. Run it.
- **Cadence: every completed job, same day, no exceptions.** The minute a job is `done`, send the ask while they're happiest. Ten jobs this week → aim for 5+ reviews. A steady drip of new reviews beats a pile of old ones.
- **Reply to every review**, and name the town in your reply ("Thanks — glad the driveway in West Windsor came out great!"). Replies are a ranking signal and the town words help you rank for that town.

## Free citations — build these, in this exact order

A **citation** is your business (NAP) listed on another site. They build prominence and feed the map pack. All free. Work down this list (highest-value first) — knock out 2–3 per week in the routine:

1. **Bing Places** — bingplaces.com. Can import straight from Google. Powers Bing + feeds some AI answers.
2. **Apple Business Connect** — businessconnect.apple.com. Puts you on Apple Maps (every iPhone). Free, high-trust.
3. **Yelp** — biz.yelp.com. Claim the free listing. Feeds Apple/Siri and a lot of "near me" results.
4. **Nextdoor Business Page** — already in `free-channels.md`; it doubles as a citation and a lead source.
5. **Facebook Page** — a real business Page (not just your profile). Fill in NAP, services, service area.
6. **Angi** (free profile) — angi.com. Claim the free listing; you don't have to pay for leads to hold the profile.
7. **Thumbtack** (free profile) — set up the profile; respond only to free, relevant leads.
8. **NJ / local directories** — Manta, Yellowpages, Hotfrog, the Plainsboro / West Windsor / Ewing chambers of commerce, township business directories, HomeAdvisor. Add a couple whenever you have 10 spare minutes.

**NAP consistency rule (non-negotiable):** Your **Name, Phone, and service area must be identical everywhere**, matching the one-pager exactly:
> **Orbit PowerWash** · **(609) 297-7412** · **orbitpowerwash.com** · serving **Plainsboro, West Windsor & Ewing, NJ**

No "Orbit Power Wash" on one site and "Orbit PowerWash" on another. No "LLC" on some and not others. No second phone number. **Inconsistent NAP splits your prominence and confuses Google about which listing is real.** When in doubt, copy-paste from `admin/company-onepager.md`.

## On-site (the website's job)

A separate agent is building **town landing pages and FAQ schema** on orbitpowerwash.com. Here's what they do and the one rule that keeps them working:

- **Town landing pages** — one page each for **Plainsboro, West Windsor, and Ewing** (e.g. /pressure-washing-plainsboro). These are what rank in **organic** results for "pressure washing [town]." Each names the town, names all five services, and speaks to that town specifically.
- **FAQ schema** — structured Q&A markup so Google can show your answers directly in results and pull them into AI answers. Same spoken-question FAQs described below.
- **The rule: every town page must be genuinely useful — not doorway spam.** A doorway page is the same text with the town name swapped; Google penalizes it and it helps no one. Each town page earns its place with real, town-specific content: the neighborhoods you cover, local surface notes (older Ewing concrete vs. newer West Windsor pavers), a real before/after from that town, honest pricing context. **If a human in that town wouldn't find the page useful, it's spam — fix it, don't ship it.**

## AEO / GEO in plain words (getting cited by AI answers)

More people now ask **Google's AI answers, ChatGPT, and similar tools** "who does pressure washing near West Windsor?" instead of scrolling links. Getting picked by those is **AEO/GEO** (answer / generative-engine optimization). It isn't a separate mystery — it's the same work, aimed at machines:

- **Write FAQ answers the way people actually ask out loud.** "How much does it cost to power wash a driveway in Plainsboro?" beats a header that just says "Driveway Pricing." Spoken-style questions with direct, complete answers are exactly what AI tools quote. Put these on the site and mirror them in GBP Q&A.
- **Structured data + llms.txt help machines read and cite you.** FAQ schema (above) and an **llms.txt** file on orbitpowerwash.com give AI tools a clean, machine-readable version of who you are, what you do, and where you work. Ask the website agent to add both.
- **Reviews get quoted by AI answers too.** AI tools summarize and quote review text when they recommend a local business — so the words *in* your reviews matter. When you ask for a review, it's fine to nudge: "if you can mention the town and what I cleaned, it helps other neighbors find me." One more reason review velocity is the whole game.

## The weekly 30-minute SEO routine

Once a week, on a slow morning. Set a timer — 30 minutes, then stop.

- [ ] **GBP photos (5 min):** upload 2–3 before/after shots from the week's jobs, captioned with town + service.
- [ ] **GBP post (3 min):** one update — a recent job, a tip, or the current offer.
- [ ] **Reviews (5 min):** confirm every `done` job this week got a same-day review ask (`lead-tracker.md`). Reply to any new review; name the town.
- [ ] **Q&A (2 min):** answer any new questions on the profile; seed one more if you're thin.
- [ ] **Citations (10 min):** claim/complete the next 1–2 on the list above. NAP copy-pasted from the one-pager.
- [ ] **Check rank (5 min):** run the incognito searches below and note where you land.

**What to check:**
- **Incognito searches** (so you see neutral results, not your own history): open a private/incognito window and search "pressure washing Plainsboro," "power washing West Windsor," "pressure washing Ewing NJ." Note whether you're in the map pack and where. Repeat weekly — you're tracking **movement**, not one snapshot.
- **GBP Insights** (in your profile dashboard): how many people found you, what they searched, and calls / clicks / direction requests. Rising calls = it's working. Watch which search terms surface you and lean into those in posts and Q&A.
