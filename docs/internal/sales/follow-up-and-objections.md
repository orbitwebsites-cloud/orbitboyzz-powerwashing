## Follow-Up Cadence

Quote sent and no reply? Follow up **twice**, then one final "last call." Then stop — three nudges is plenty, and stopping keeps you looking confident, not desperate.

### 24 hours after quote — the bump
> Hey [NAME], just making sure my quote came through — $[PRICE] for the [SERVICE]. Happy to answer any questions. I've still got **[DAY]** open if you want it. 👍

### 72 hours after quote — the value nudge
> Hi [NAME] — still happy to get your [SERVICE] taken care of. Quick heads up: I'm booking up my first week fast, and the **founding-customer 20% off** is only for my first 10 customers. Want me to hold a spot for you?

### Final "last call" — founding offer expiring
Send this ~24–48h after the 72h nudge, or end of week one, whichever comes first.
> Last call on this, [NAME] — my **founding-customer 20% off** wraps up [this weekend / when I hit 10 jobs]. After that the [SERVICE] goes back to $[FULL PRICE] instead of $[DISCOUNTED PRICE]. If you want in at the launch rate, just reply "yes" and I'll lock **[DAY]**. Either way, thanks!

> After the last-call message, stop following up. If they come back later, welcome them warmly — just at regular pricing.

## The 8 Common Objections

Keep every reply text-length. Acknowledge → one honest point → easy next step.

### 1. "Too expensive"
> Totally fair to check. That price is for a proper job — right chemistry for your surface, done carefully, not a rushed blast-and-go. If budget's the thing, I can do just the [most important piece] for less to start. Want me to price that?

### 2. "I'll do it myself"
> Respect that! Honestly, for a driveway a rental washer can do okay. Where DIY gets people is soft-washing siding — wrong pressure or mix and you can streak or damage it. If you'd rather not risk it, I've got you. Offer stands whenever. 👍

### 3. "Need to ask my spouse"
> Makes sense — go for it. I'll hold **[DAY]** for you for 24 hours so you don't lose the spot or the founding-customer rate. Just text me a "yes" or "no" when you know. No pressure either way.

### 4. "Maybe next month"
> No problem at all. One thing to flag: the **founding-customer 20% off** is only good for my first 10 customers, so next month it'll likely be full price. If you want to lock the discount now, I can even schedule you out a couple weeks. Want me to?

### 5. "Are you insured?"
> **[CONFIRM YOUR INSURANCE STATUS BEFORE USING — fill in your real, true answer below. Do not send a claim you can't back up.]**
>
> - If insured: *"Yes — I carry [general liability] insurance and I'm happy to send proof before I start. Peace of mind matters."*
> - If not yet insured: *"Great question. I'm a local owner-operator and I treat every property like my own — I'm [in the process of finalizing / about to bind] my liability coverage. Happy to talk it through so you're comfortable."*
>
> Never claim coverage you don't have. Fill this in with the truth before quoting anyone.

### 6. "You're new, no reviews"
> 100% true — I'm just launching, and that's exactly why you get **20% off** as a founding customer. You're getting my full attention and a guy who badly wants your review to be great. I'll send before/after photos and you only pay when you're happy with it.

### 7. "Can you do it cheaper?"
> I keep my pricing honest, so I don't have a lot of padding to cut — but you've already got the **founding-customer 20% off** built into that number, which is the best rate I'll offer. If you want to trim it, I can drop a service from the job. What works for your budget?

### 8. "I already have a guy"
> Nice — a good washer is worth keeping! If yours ever can't fit you in or you want a second quote, keep my number: (609) 297-7412. And if you refer me to a neighbor while I'm running my founding-customer deal, I'll take care of you on your next job. 👍

## Golden rules
- **Never argue price.** Acknowledge, reframe the value once, offer an easy yes.
- **The founding discount is your urgency.** It's real and it expires — that's what moves "maybe" to "yes."
- **Three touches max**, then stop.
- **Be honest about being new.** It's a strength here — turn it into the reason to book.
