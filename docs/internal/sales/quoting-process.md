Solo operator, quoting from a phone, mostly by text. **Speed wins.** The fastest fair quote usually gets the job.

## The One Rule: Respond within 15 minutes (8am–8pm)

Leads go cold fast. If a text or website lead comes in between **8am and 8pm**, reply within **15 minutes**. Outside those hours, reply first thing next morning with a quick apology for the hour. First responder usually wins the job — beat the "I'll get back to you next week" competition.

---

## Step 1 — Qualify (4 questions max)

Don't interrogate. Ask these four, ideally in one message. Always ask for **photos** — they're how you quote without driving out.

1. **Address?** (lets you check driveway size on Google Maps satellite view)
2. **What are we cleaning?** (driveway / house / patio — or a combo)
3. **Surface material?** (concrete, pavers, wood/composite deck, vinyl or other siding)
4. **Any problem spots?** (oil stains, heavy algae/green, mildew) — **"a few photos would let me quote you fast."**

### Template — first reply to a text lead
> Hey, thanks for reaching out — this is [YOUR NAME] with Orbit PowerWash. I can get you a price fast. Quick questions:
> 1) What's the address?
> 2) What are we cleaning — driveway, house, patio?
> 3) What's the surface (concrete, pavers, vinyl siding, wood deck)?
> 4) Any problem spots like oil stains or heavy green?
>
> If you can text me a couple photos, I can usually quote you within the hour. 👍

---

## Step 2 — Quote from photos + maps (don't insist on a visit)

**Default to a remote quote.** You can price the large majority of residential jobs without leaving your driveway:

- **Driveway size:** measure it on **Google Maps satellite view** (drop on the address, use the built-in "measure distance" tool) and confirm with a **street view** glance for layout.
- **House size/stories:** street view + the customer's photos give you stories, siding type, and elevations.
- **Condition & problem areas:** the photos you asked for in Step 1.

Match what you see to the **Pricing Guide** tiers. Pick the **"Quote this" default**, then apply the **Founding-Customer 20%** during week one.

## Step 3 — Send the text quote

Keep it short: **price → what's included → next available day → one-line booking CTA.** No essays.

## Step 4 — When an in-person look IS required

Most jobs quote remotely. Go see it first only when:

- **3-story home** or hard roof/ladder access questions.
- **Heavy oil / mystery staining** you can't judge from a photo.
- **Big, unclear scope** (multiple structures, commercial-ish, "the whole property").
- Customer flatly **won't send photos** and the job's big enough to be worth the drive.

### Template — offering a quick look
> Happy to swing by and give you an exact price — takes me 10 minutes and there's no charge. I'm near [AREA] on **[DAY]**. Does morning or afternoon work better?

## Step 5 — Book it (one message)

When they say yes, confirm **day + arrival window + water spigot access** in a single message. The spigot line matters — you run off the customer's outdoor water.

### Template — booking confirmation
> You're booked! 🎉
>
> **[SERVICE] — $[PRICE]**
> **[DAY, DATE]**, arriving between **[WINDOW, e.g. 9–11am]**
>
> Two quick things so I'm ready to roll:
> • I run off your outdoor water spigot — can you make sure it's **turned on and reachable**?
> • Please have **cars off the driveway** and **windows closed** that morning.
>
> Text me anytime at (609) 297-7412. See you [DAY]!

1. Lead in → reply in **15 min** (8am–8pm)
2. Ask the **4 questions** + request photos
3. Quote from **photos + Google Maps** (skip the visit when you can)
