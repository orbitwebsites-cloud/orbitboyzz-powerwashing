It signals careful, not cheap.

## Bundle Discounts

Stack the visit — one trip, one setup, more revenue. **Bundle services are Driveway, House, Patio & Deck, and Fence.** **Trash bin cleaning never counts as a bundle service** — it's an add-on priced on top (see section 5), not part of the discount.

| Bundle | Discount |
|---|---|
| Any 2 services | **10% off** the combined total |
| Any 3 or more services | **15% off** the combined total |

**Example:** Colonial house ($450) + 2-car driveway ($150) = $600 → **10% off = $540**. Add a fence ($300) and that's 3 services → **15% off**. Toss two bins on top at **$40** — bins are never discounted.

**20% off — first 10 customers only.** This is the launch offer. Use it, and be transparent about why:

> "You'd be one of my first 10 customers, so I'm taking 20% off — I want a few great jobs and honest reviews to launch on."

**Rules:**
- **Does not stack with bundle discounts.** Apply whichever is larger (the 20% founding discount almost always wins). Never apply both.
- Applies to the **"Quote this" default**, not the bottom of the range. Discount off a fair price, not off a floor price.
- **Minimum still holds:** never let a founding-discounted job fall below the **$150 minimum**.
- **Bin add-ons are excluded.** Never discount trash bin cleaning — it's already small and favor-priced (see section 5). The founding discount applies only to the real services.
- It expires. Say "this week" or "for my first 10 jobs" so it has a real edge (see follow-up doc).

**Example:** Standard colonial default $450 → founding 20% off = **$360**. Still a solid solo half-day. Fair, not desperate.

## When to Walk Away or Price Up

1. **Heavy oil / transmission stains on a driveway.** Organic staining cleans up; deep petroleum stains often don't come 100% out. Never promise a spotless result. Add a **$50–$100 degreasing surcharge** and set expectations in writing:
   > "I can knock these way down, but deep oil stains rarely come out 100% — I'll get it as clean as it'll go. There's a $[50–100] degreasing add-on for that."
   If they expect perfection, walk.

2. **3-story homes.** Soft-washing a full 3rd story from the ground is a different job — reach, safety, and control all change. Either **price up meaningfully** (start at the top of the "larger" range + $150 and quote in person) or decline if it's beyond your current setup. Don't undercharge yourself into a risky job.

3. **Roof cleaning asked for.** **We don't do roofs.** It's a safety and liability line we don't cross right now. Decline plainly and refer out — it keeps trust:
   > "I don't do roofs — not something I take on. I can point you to someone who does. Happy to handle your house siding, driveway, or patio though."
