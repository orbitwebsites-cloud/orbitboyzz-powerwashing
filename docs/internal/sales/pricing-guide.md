## When to Walk Away or Price Up

1. **Heavy oil / transmission stains on a driveway.** Organic staining cleans up; deep petroleum stains often don't come 100% out. Never promise a spotless result. Add a **$50–$100 degreasing surcharge** and set expectations in writing:
   > "I can knock these way down, but deep oil stains rarely come out 100% — I'll get it as clean as it'll go. There's a $[50–100] degreasing add-on for that."
   If they expect perfection, walk.

2. **3-story homes.** Soft-washing a full 3rd story from the ground is a different job — reach, safety, and control all change. Either **price up meaningfully** (start at the top of the "larger" range + $150 and quote in person) or decline if it's beyond your current setup. Don't undercharge yourself into a risky job.

3. **Roof cleaning asked for.** **We don't do roofs.** It's a safety and liability line we don't cross right now. Decline plainly and refer out — it keeps trust:
   > "I don't do roofs — not something I take on. I can point you to someone who does. Happy to handle your house siding, driveway, or patio though."
